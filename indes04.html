Ukraine hits Russia with missile and drone strikes

         

The apparent Ukrainian attacks come the day after at least 39 civilians
were killed in Russian strikes on Ukraine.

Thirty-two Ukrainian drones were detected over Russia, Moscow officials reported on Saturday, a day after an
18-hour aerial barrage across Ukraine killed at least 39 civilians.The Kremlin accused Ukrainian forces of also
shelling Belgorod, killing 10 people and injuring 45. Two children were among the dead in the Russian border
city, regional governor Vyacheslav Gladkov said in a statement on social media.

Israeli tanks advance deeper into Gaza districts, 12 weeks into war

         

A reported attack on a relief convoy returning from a delivery in Gaza has put heightened attention on the obstacles to supplying humanitarian aid in the territory. UNRWA, the U.N. agency for Palestinian refugees, said Friday on social media that Israeli soldiers had fired at the marked U.N. trucks, writing “aid workers should never be a target.” The IDF did not respond immediately to a request for comment. The workers were responding to an increasingly desperate need for supplies in Gaza, which is facing shortages of food, water and electricity and receiving a fraction of the aid that it did before the war, humanitarian groups say.The Kerem Shalom crossing at Gaza’s border with Israel reopened Friday after closing because of “significant security incidents” in its vicinity, the U.N. humanitarian agency said in an update. Eighty-one trucks carrying food and medicine entered Gaza through Kerem Shalom and Rafah on Friday, the agency said, calling the volume “woefully inadequate.”
